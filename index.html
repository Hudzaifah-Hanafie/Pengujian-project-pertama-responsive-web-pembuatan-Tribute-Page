Yuan Longping

Globally renowned agronomist & Father of hybrid rice

Yuan Longping in 1962.

Here's a time line of Yuan Longping's life:

  • 1930 (Sep 7)

    - Born at Peking Union Medical College Hospital in Beiping (now Beijing), China

  • 1949–1953

    - Studied agronomy at Southwest Agricultural College (now Southwest University); graduated in 1953

  • 1953

    - Began teaching at Anjiang Agricultural School in Hunan Province

  • 1960

    - Motivated by the Great Famine, began focusing on hybrid rice; discovered a wild hybrid rice stalk

  • 1964 (Jul 5)

    - Identified a naturally male-sterile rice plant, enabling large-scale hybrid breeding

  • 1966 (Feb)

    - Published his first major paper on rice male sterility in Science Bulletin

  • 1972–1973

    - Led national research efforts; developed the first “three-line” hybrid rice system; Oct 1973 published success

  • 1974

    - First high-yield hybrid rice variety (“Nanyou 2”) achieved 20% higher yields than traditional varieties

  • 1975–1976

    - Statewide adoption began; by 1976, demonstration covered 2.08 million mu; by 1988 hybrid rice covered ~39.6% of rice fields

  • 1979

    - Hybrid rice technology introduced to the U.S., marking China’s first IP transfer abroad

  • 1981

    - Awarded China's first National Special Invention Award for indica hybrid rice

  • 1987

    - His team discovered photoperiod-sensitive male sterility, enabling more efficient “two-line” hybrid system

  • 1991

    - Became chief consultant to the UN’s FAO

  • 1995

    - Elected to the Chinese Academy of Engineering

  • 2000

    - Received China’s State Preeminent Science and Technology Award

  • 2004

    - Awarded both the Wolf Prize in Agriculture and the World Food Prize

  • 2006

    - Elected foreign associate of the U.S. National Academy of Sciences

  • 2008

    - Carried the Olympic torch in Changsha

  • 2011

    - Won the Mahathir Science Award

  • 2012–2013

    - Developed “super‑hybrid” rice with yields approaching 1 ton per mu; 2013 variety hit 988.1 kg/mu

  • 2017

    - Set new world record: 1,149 kg/mu

  • 2019

    - Awarded the Medal (Order) of the Republic, China’s highest honor

  • 2020–2021

    - Advanced “third-generation” hybrid rice to annual yields of ~1,500 kg/mu; worked on salt-tolerant rice initiatives

  • 2021 (May 22)

    - Dies at age of 90

China’s Ministry of Agriculture and Rural Affairs called his passing “a great loss to the country’s agriculture,” praising Yuan’s noble spirit and vowing to uphold his work in ensuring food security.

"He devoted his life to the research of hybrid rice, helping billions achieve food security" -- Qu Dongyu, director-general of the UN Food and Agriculture Organization

"A few words will suffice to remark on Yuan's achievements: He helped the world fend off hunger" -- Hu Peisong, director-general of the China National Rice Research Institute
